Delver

Delver is a top down dungeon exploration game programmed in C#. This was made for a project in my first year of college. While the assignment was to make a topdown game where you attacked by walking into enemies, I decided to go beyond that and explore procedural dungeon generation. The game was later modified by a classmate of mine.

My Role

As the main designer and programmer of this project, I programmed the player, the enemies, the map generation, and most of what you see in the game. In our second year, a classmate of mine added a shopkeeper and quest system into the game.

The Dungeon Generation

I couldn't make the map too big or else it would take the player too long to explore each floor, so I had to ensure the map was dense while keeping the size limited to five rooms by five. When the game needs to generate a floor, it assigns a random type of room to every other placement on the five by five grid in a checkerboard pattern. These room types determine which of the room's walls have doors. From there it fills in the rest of the rooms based off of the room types around it to makes sure that no doors lead to empty space. Finally the game checks if any rooms are inaccessible from the center room, removing the rooms if they are. The center room, where the player spawns, will always have a door in each of the four walls.
