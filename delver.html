Delver

Made in C#

Delver is a top down dungeon exploration game programmed in C#. This was made in my first year of college. I was tasked with making a topdown game where you attacked by walking into enemies, I decided to go beyond that and explore procedural dungeon generation. The game was later modified by a classmate of mine who added a shop system and a quest system.

What I Did

I was the primary developer for Delver, aside from the previously mentioned additions made by a classmate much later.

  • Designed and implemented a random dungeon generation system.
  • Created an enemy manager and a modular enemy design to allow for easy expansion.
  • Designed and implemented a minimap that fills in as you explore the dungeon.

    • Dungeon Generation

    The map's max size was limited due to the scale of the game and the player's speed, as having a map that was too big would make exploration tedious rather than exciting. I also wanted to have every generated map to be dense and full, using as much of the space available as it can. The basic steps taken to generate the map are as follows:

    1. Create a checkerboard pattern of undetermined rooms to figure out what walls will have doors. This will ensure each map has a minimum level of complexity.
    2. Fill in the gaps in the checkerboard making sure all doors are connected to another door.
    3. Remove any room spaces that can't be accessed from the center room.
    4. Fill the room spaces in with a random room from the text file that matches its doors.
